SECTION 1: UNIVERSITY — GENERAL INFO
==============================================

Q: What is KIIT?
A: KIIT stands for Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology. It is a Deemed to be University located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India. It was established as an institution in 1997 and declared a Deemed University in 2004 under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956.

Q: Who is the founder of KIIT?
A: Prof. (Dr.) Achyuta Samanta is the founder of KIIT, KISS, and KIMS. He is an eminent educationist, philanthropist, and social worker who transformed KIIT from a small institution into a globally recognized university.

Q: Where is KIIT located?
A: KIIT is located in Bhubaneswar-751024, Odisha, India. The campus spans 30 campuses across a 6 km x 6 km (36 sq. km) radius, with a total built-up area of 2.5 crore sq. ft.

Q: What is KIIT's world ranking?
A: As per the latest rankings:
- Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings 2026: 501-600
- THE Asia University Rankings 2025: 184th in Asia, 8th in India
- THE Impact Rankings 2025: 101-200 overall, 5th in India
- NIRF India Rankings 2025: 17th among Government and Private Universities
- QS Asia University Rankings 2026: 294th in Asia, 1st in Odisha

Q: What programmes does the School of Computer Engineering offer?
A: The School of Computer Engineering offers:
- B.Tech in Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
- B.Tech in Information Technology (IT)
- B.Tech in Computer Science & Communication Engineering (CS&CE)
- B.Tech in Computer Science & System Engineering (CS&SE)
- M.Tech

Q: Is KIIT a residential university?
A: Yes. KIIT is fully residential. All students are required to reside in and be members of a hostel assigned to them. Under special circumstances, the Director may permit a student to reside with their Parent/Guardian on or near campus.

Q: What is the medium of instruction at KIIT?
A: The medium of instruction, examination, and project reports at KIIT is English.

Q: What is the Tutor-Mentor system at KIIT?
A: Each student is assigned a dedicated faculty member called a Tutor-Mentor. The mentor assists with registration, tracks attendance and performance, communicates with parents when needed, monitors health and well-being, guides campus conduct, and helps with grievance redressal.

SECTION 2: ADMISSIONS
==============================================

Q: How do I get admission to KIIT B.Tech?
A: Admission to B.Tech at KIIT is done through KIITEE — the KIIT Joint Entrance Examination. Based on your KIITEE rank, you are called for a Single Window Counseling (SWC) process where you can select your branch of preference.

Q: What is KIITEE?
A: KIITEE stands for KIIT Joint Entrance Examination. It is the entrance exam conducted by KIIT for admission to its B.Tech, M.Tech, MCA, and other programmes. The official website is www.kiitee.ac.in.

Q: Is lateral entry available at KIIT?
A: Yes. B.Tech Lateral Entry is available for 3 years (6 semesters) for Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, Aerospace, Mechatronics, Mechanical (Automobile), Electronics & Telecommunication, Electronics & Computer Science Engineering, and Electronics and Electrical Engineering.

Q: What documents are required for KIIT admission?
A: Required documents typically include mark sheets, transfer certificate, character certificate, domicile/nationality proof, passport-size photographs, and caste certificate if applicable. The complete checklist is provided during the SWC counseling process.

Q: What is Single Window Counseling (SWC)?
A: Single Window Counseling (SWC) is the counseling process conducted by KIIT after KIITEE results. Students are called based on their KIITEE rank and can select their preferred branch of B.Tech during this process.

Q: Can foreign students apply to KIIT?
A: Yes. KIIT admits international students. There is a dedicated international hostel on campus. Refunds for foreign student candidates are made in accordance with Foreign Exchange Regulations. Contact: dean.isr@kiit.ac.in

Q: What is the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFW)?
A: Under TFW, seats up to a maximum of 5% of the sanctioned B.Tech intake are available for students holding BPL (Below Poverty Line) cards. These seats are supernumerary. The waiver covers tuition fees only — all other fees must be paid by the student.

Q: What is the maximum duration to complete B.Tech at KIIT?
A: The maximum permitted duration to complete all B.Tech degree requirements is 12 registered semesters. If a student opts for the slow-paced programme, this is extended by 2 more semesters (14 total). Summer semesters do not count as registered semesters.

Q: Can I take a semester break at KIIT?
A: Yes. A student may be permitted by the Dean (Academics) to take a semester break or temporary withdrawal for reasons of ill health or other valid reasons. A maximum of 2 semesters (continuous or intermittent) can be taken as break during the entire program.

Q: What happens if my program is terminated due to backlogs?
A: You may appeal to the Chairperson of the Academic Council through your Tutor-Mentor and Dean (Academics) for a second chance to re-enroll. The Chairperson may grant readmission on behalf of the Academic Council.

SECTION 4: EXAM STRUCTURE & GRADING
==============================================

Q: How is a theory course assessed at KIIT?
A: A theory course is assessed as follows:
- Activities (quizzes, assignments, projects, viva, etc.): 30%
- Mid-Semester Examination (approx. 1 hour): 20%
- End-Semester Examination (approx. 3 hours): 50%

Q: How is a practical/laboratory course assessed?
A: Practical courses are assessed as:
- Continuous assessment: 60%
- End-semester exam: 40%

Q: How are sessional courses assessed?
A: Sessional courses are assessed 100% in class — no separate end-semester exam.

Q: What is the grading system at KIIT?
A: The grade-to-score mapping is:
- O (Outstanding): 90-100 → Grade Point 10
- E (Excellent): 80-89 → Grade Point 9
- A (Very Good): 70-79 → Grade Point 8
- B (Good): 60-69 → Grade Point 7
- C (Average): 50-59 → Grade Point 6
- D (Below Average): 40-49 → Grade Point 5
- F (Fail): Below 40 → Grade Point 0
- I: Incomplete (temporary grade)

Q: What is SGPA?
A: SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) is the weighted average of grade points across all courses in a semester. Formula: SGPA = Σ(C × GP) / ΣC, where C is the credit of a subject and GP is the grade point earned. F-grade subjects are included; I-grade subjects are excluded.

Q: What is CGPA?
A: CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the weighted average of grade points across all semesters completed so far, calculated using the same formula as SGPA but over the entire program duration.

Q: What is the I grade and what happens if it is not resolved?
A: The I (Incomplete) grade is a temporary grade assigned when a student has not completed a small part of the course but has done satisfactory work otherwise. The student must complete the remaining work within one month of the start of the next regular semester. If not resolved within this deadline, the I grade automatically converts to an F grade.

Q: What is the minimum attendance required to sit for end-semester exams?
A: Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each course. If attendance falls below 75%, the student is debarred from the end-semester examination and awarded an I grade in that course.

Q: How is attendance calculated?
A: Every scheduled lecture, tutorial, or practical class counts as one unit, regardless of the number of contact hours in that session.

Q: Can I apply for a make-up exam if I miss the mid-semester exam?
A: Yes. If you miss the mid-semester exam for valid reasons, you can request a make-up exam from the course instructor within 10 days of the missed examination. The instructor will schedule the make-up exam during designated slots in the Academic Calendar or at mutual convenience, subject to their satisfaction with your explanation.

Q: What is the minimum CGPA required to graduate with a B.Tech degree?
A: You must secure a CGPA of 6.0 or higher at the end of the semester in which you complete all degree requirements. You must also have a minimum D grade in every theory subject and a minimum C grade in every practical and sessional subject.

Q: What is the supplementary examination?
A: The supplementary examination is held annually at the end of each academic year for 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th year students, before the start of the next academic session. It is for failed theory subjects only, for the current academic year.

Q: How many subjects can I appear for in the supplementary exam?
A: You can appear for a maximum of 50% (ceiling value) of the total theory subjects of the current academic year (both Autumn and Spring semesters combined).

Q: Is there a grade penalty in the supplementary exam?
A: Yes. You will ordinarily receive one grade lower than your actual scored grade in the supplementary exam, subject to a minimum of C grade (i.e., C grade remains unchanged). However, there is NO grade penalty if you attend summer term classes and satisfy the attendance criteria.

Q: Can I use the supplementary exam to improve my grade?
A: Yes. You can appear in the supplementary exam to improve grades in a maximum of 3 theory subjects where you currently have a C or D grade, and there will be no grade reduction in this case.

Q: How many subjects can I register for grade improvement during a regular semester?
A: You can register fresh in a maximum of 3 theory subjects (where you have already passed) for grade improvement. The formula is: N = 3 − (number of current backlog subjects). The final grade is the best between your original and improvement attempt.

Q: What are the promotion criteria from one year to the next?
A: To be promoted from year i to year (i+1), you must:
1. Have no more than 5 backlog subjects in the current academic year (both semesters combined).
2. Have no backlogs from any year below the current year.
3. Not be under suspension or denied regular student privileges at the time of promotion.

Q: What is the minimum degree requirement for B.Tech?
A: You must:
1. Complete all credit requirements with a minimum D grade in every theory and minimum C grade in every practical/sessional.
2. Achieve a CGPA of 6.0 or higher.
3. Clear all institutional and hostel dues.
4. Have no pending disciplinary action.

SECTION 6: COURSE CURRICULUM & REGISTRATION
==============================================

Q: What are the total credits required for B.Tech CSE?
A: The total minimum credits required for B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering is 162 credits (as per the KIITDUCSE22 curriculum).

Q: What are the credit categories for B.Tech at KIIT?
A: Credits are distributed across:
- Humanities and Social Sciences (HASS): 12 credits
- Basic Sciences (BS): 30 credits
- Engineering Sciences (ES): 19 credits
- Program Core (PC): 59 credits
- Program Electives (PE): 15 credits
- Open Electives: 6 credits
- Projects/Internship: 14 credits
Total: 155 credits (standard B.Tech)

Q: How are credits assigned to courses at KIIT?
A: Credits are assigned as follows:
- 1 credit for each lecture/tutorial period per week.
- 0.5 credit per hour for each Laboratory, Practical, Project, or Sessional.

Q: What is a Minor discipline and how can I opt for it?
A: A Minor allows you to gain expertise in an area outside your major B.Tech discipline. You need to complete an additional 20 credits from that minor's course basket (6 credits from Open Electives can be counted). Eligibility: CGPA of 7.5 or above after the 4th semester. Applications are submitted at the end of the 5th semester. Minor courses run from the 6th to 8th semester. The minor is mentioned on your degree certificate.

Q: What is a B.Tech (Honours) degree?
A: B.Tech (Honours) requires 9 additional credits beyond the standard degree. Eligibility: CGPA of 8.0 or above after the 5th semester. Applications are submitted at the end of the 6th semester. Honours courses run in the 7th and 8th semesters and are mentioned on your degree certificate.

Q: What are Open Electives?
A: Open Electives allow you to choose subjects offered by any School at KIIT, enabling you to pursue interests outside your branch and work toward earning a Minor. They are offered in Semesters 5 through 8.

Q: What is K-Explore?
A: K-Explore is a practice-based Open Elective offered in Semester 5. It considers students' co-curricular and extra-curricular activities for evaluation and provides flexibility in learning.

Q: What is a Vocational Elective?
A: Vocational Electives (offered in Semester 3) give engineering students deeper appreciation of practical aspects of engineering by relating theoretical knowledge to practical skills. One vocational elective must be chosen from the options announced each semester.

Q: How do I register for courses each semester?
A: All students must register/enroll at the beginning of each semester through the online SAP portal of KIIT on the date specified in the Academic Calendar. A student with backlogs must register for the oldest backlog courses first.

Q: What happens if I miss the registration date?
A: If you miss the specified registration date, the Dean may allow you to enroll within one week with a fine. After one week, you need special permission from the Chairperson of the Academic Council through your Tutor-Mentor and Dean.

Q: What is the internship requirement in B.Tech?
A: As part of the B.Tech curriculum, students must complete an industrial internship in India for a minimum of 4 weeks. It must be done during the summer vacation after the 4th semester or any subsequent vacation before the final semester. On successful completion, 2 credits are awarded.

Q: What is a semester exchange program at KIIT?
A: KIIT has MoUs with eminent academic institutions in India and abroad for semester exchange. Selected students can go on exchange visits for 1 or 2 semesters. Credits and grades earned abroad are converted to KIIT equivalents as per Academic Council approved criteria.

Q: What is the Summer Vacation Semester for?
A: The Summer Vacation Semester is offered to students who wish to clear backlog courses or spend a semester off-campus. No student can register for more than 3 courses in a summer semester. A fee is charged per credit registered. Withdrawal from summer courses is not permitted.

SECTION 9: DISCIPLINE & CONDUCT
==============================================

Q: What are the major disciplinary offences at KIIT?
A: Major offences include ragging, sexual harassment, violence, use of weapons, possession of drugs/alcohol/tobacco, gambling, plagiarism, copying in examinations, forging documents, vandalism, and violations of the Indian Penal Code.

Q: What is the punishment for ragging at KIIT?
A: Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited. It can lead to expulsion (permanent dismissal) from the University. All students and guardians must sign an undertaking against ragging at the time of admission.

Q: What are minor sanctions for disciplinary violations?
A: Minor sanctions include: written warning, tendering an apology, suspension of privileges (events, placements, lab access), hostel changes or expulsion from hostel, fines up to Rs. 10,000, and community service hours.

Q: What are major sanctions for serious disciplinary violations?
A: Major sanctions include: debarring from examinations, course/semester drop, rustication for a specified period, restitution (monetary compensation for damages), forfeiture of caution deposit, and expulsion (permanent dismissal from the University).

Q: How do I appeal against a disciplinary action?
A: A student aggrieved by any disciplinary action can appeal to the Director (Student Affairs) within two weeks with proper justification. Any student aggrieved by the Director (SA) can appeal to the Pro-Vice Chancellor. The decision of the Pro-Vice Chancellor is final and binding.

Q: Is plagiarism allowed at KIIT?
A: No. Plagiarism in any form is strictly prohibited at KIIT. The library provides access to Turnitin anti-plagiarism software. Academic plagiarism is treated as an examination malpractice.

Q: What is KIIT Quest?
A: KIIT Quest is an app built by the KIIT NEXUS team. It is live on the Google Play Store and helps KIIT students with exam preparation through Previous Year Questions (PYQs) and study materials. Future versions will include Placement Analytics.

Q: What is kiitnexus.in?
A: kiitnexus.in is the official live platform of KIIT NEXUS used for internal assessments, progress tracking, competitions, and leaderboards for community members.

Q: What domains can I join in KIIT NEXUS?
A: Technical domains: Web Development, Android Development, Flutter, Machine Learning.
Non-technical domains: Graphic Designing, Broadcasting, Marketing, Operations.

Q: What will I achieve by the end of 2nd year if I join KIIT NEXUS?
A: By the end of your 2nd year, you will have:
- At least 2 live, deployable projects
- 1 research-based or innovative project
- An industry-ready GitHub portfolio
- Resume-ready impact statements for placements

Q: What placement preparation does KIIT NEXUS offer?
A: KIIT NEXUS integrates DSA (Data Structures and Algorithms), aptitude, reasoning, and Core CS modules (OS, DBMS, Computer Networks, OOP) into its roadmap to make every member placement-ready by 3rd year.
